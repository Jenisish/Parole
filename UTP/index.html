Q&A - Undertrial Prisoners

Q1: What are undertrial prisoners?

A1: Undertrial prisoners are individuals who have been arrested and are awaiting trial or the completion of legal proceedings. They have not been convicted of a crime.

Q2: What rights do undertrial prisoners have?

A2: Undertrial prisoners have the right to a fair and speedy trial, legal representation, and humane treatment while in custody. They are considered innocent until proven guilty.
